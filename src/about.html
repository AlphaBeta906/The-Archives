We are the Archivists. We are a group of scholars dedicated to the preservation, research, and documentation of historica and event prediction.

Our History

On circa 1775, our great leader Altair, was transported to the Hub of Worlds. There, he met Nayáquille, Patron God of Learning and Knowledge, and God of Wisdom, Creativity and Arts. There he experienced an epiphany and the discovery of event prediction. He changed his name to Altair, and thus he founded the Great Society of the Archivists.

On December 4th, 1775, the Urpadine Creed was signed by Altair, Great Disciple Tang En, Great Disciple Silcritus of Drivorae, and Great Disciple James Maxmillian.

Our Objectives

This section talks about the objectives of the Great Society of the Archivists. To see our objectives regarding The Archives, see: Our Objectives

Our objectives as the Great Society of the Archivists, were drafted in the Urpadine Creed, as is as follows:

  • Preserve as much historica as possible.
  • Develop the theory of event prediction.
  • Build temples and churches in belief of Nayáquille.
  • Provoke a sense of wonder, appreciation and curiosity regarding event prediction.
